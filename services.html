Focus Areas · Autonomous uncrewed systems

The problem is clear.
The solution is what we are building.

Modern uncrewed aerial platforms can fly. What they cannot do reliably is think - navigate without GPS, perceive without a datalink, and make decisions without a human in the loop. These are the problems Vyomaryx was founded to solve, for the Army, Navy, and Air Force.

The Gap We Are Closing

What happens when the signal dies?

In contested environments - where GPS is jammed, communication links are severed, and the electromagnetic spectrum is weaponised - most uncrewed systems become inert. They loiter. They return to base. They wait for a human instruction that may never come.

This is the fundamental limitation of remotely piloted aircraft: they are extensions of a human operator, not independent agents. The moment the link is cut, the mission ends.

Vyomaryx exists to close this gap - so that an uncrewed platform can continue its mission and make sensible decisions even when it is entirely alone in a contested sky. How we do this is the heart of our work, and it is still taking shape.

Focus Area 01

Autonomous navigation in GPS-denied environments

The Indian armed forces operate across terrains - high-altitude borders, maritime zones, urban theatres - where GPS is either unavailable or actively denied by an adversary. Current platforms have no reliable fallback.

Vyomaryx is developing onboard intelligence that lets an uncrewed platform hold its position and continue a mission without depending on an external signal. The technical approach is proprietary and under active development.

The problem we are solving

GPS-denied, jammed, and spoofed environments render most uncrewed platforms mission-ineffective today.

Focus Area 02

Onboard perception and autonomous decision-making

A platform that can navigate is only half the solution. It must also be able to perceive its environment and act on that perception without waiting for a human instruction that may not arrive in time.

Vyomaryx is developing onboard intelligence that allows a platform to assess a situation and act independently when a link or operator is unavailable. The technical approach is proprietary and under active development.

The problem we are solving

Perception and decision-making today depend on a datalink and a human. Both fail under contested conditions.

Our Approach

Built for India. Owned by India.

Principle 01

Edge-first, always

Every model we build is designed to run on the aircraft itself. If it cannot operate without a connection, it does not belong on a contested platform.

Principle 02

Sovereign technology

Designed, trained, and maintained in India. No foreign dependency in the stack. The armed forces get full transparency into what they are operating.

Principle 03

Platform-agnostic

We are building an intelligence layer, not an airframe. Our software is designed to integrate with existing and future uncrewed platforms across all three services.
